# English — Complete Rules

## Homophones & Commonly Confused Words

### Critical pairs (NEVER confuse)

| Word A | Word B | Rule |
|--------|--------|------|
| its | it's | its = possessive ("its color"); it's = "it is" ("it's raining") |
| their | there / they're | their = possessive; there = place; they're = "they are" |
| your | you're | your = possessive; you're = "you are" |
| who's | whose | who's = "who is"; whose = possessive |
| affect | effect | affect = verb (to influence); effect = noun (the result). Exception: "effect change" (verb = bring about) |
| then | than | then = time sequence; than = comparison |
| lose | loose | lose = verb (to misplace); loose = adjective (not tight) |
| ensure | insure | ensure = make certain; insure = financial insurance |
| complement | compliment | complement = complete/enhance; compliment = praise |
| principal | principle | principal = main/head person; principle = rule/belief |
| stationary | stationery | stationary = not moving; stationery = paper/writing materials |
| accept | except | accept = receive; except = exclude |
| advice | advise | advice = noun; advise = verb |
| practice | practise | practice = noun (AmE: both); practise = verb (BrE) |
| license | licence | license = verb (AmE: both); licence = noun (BrE) |

### Less obvious but important

| Word A | Word B | Rule |
|--------|--------|------|
| fewer | less | fewer = countable ("fewer items"); less = uncountable ("less water") |
| farther | further | farther = physical distance; further = figurative/additional |
| which | that | which = non-restrictive (with comma); that = restrictive (no comma) |
| lay | lie | lay = transitive (lay the book down); lie = intransitive (lie down) |
| who | whom | who = subject; whom = object ("To whom it may concern") |
| imply | infer | imply = suggest (speaker); infer = deduce (listener) |
| disinterested | uninterested | disinterested = impartial; uninterested = not interested |
| comprise | compose | "The team comprises five members" / "Five members compose the team" |
| continual | continuous | continual = repeated; continuous = uninterrupted |

## Punctuation

### Oxford comma (serial comma)
Use consistently throughout a document. Pick one style and stick with it.

**With Oxford comma (recommended):**
"We need HTML, CSS, and JavaScript."

**Without Oxford comma:**
"We need HTML, CSS and JavaScript."

**Why it matters — ambiguity:**
- "I love my parents, Batman and Wonder Woman." (parents are superheroes?)
- "I love my parents, Batman, and Wonder Woman." (three separate entities — clear)

### Em dash vs en dash vs hyphen
- **Em dash** (—): parenthetical statements, interruptions. "The result — surprisingly — was positive."
- **En dash** (–): ranges. "Pages 10–15", "2020–2025"
- **Hyphen** (-): compound words. "well-known", "up-to-date"
- NEVER use double hyphens (--) as a substitute for em dash

### Quotation marks
- **American English**: "double" for primary, 'single' for nested
- **British English**: 'single' for primary, "double" for nested
- Periods and commas go INSIDE quotation marks (AmE)
- Colons and semicolons go OUTSIDE quotation marks

### Apostrophes
- Possessive singular: "the user's data"
- Possessive plural: "the users' data"
- It's = "it is" (contraction), its = possessive (NO apostrophe)
- Years: "the 1990s" (NO apostrophe), "the '90s" (apostrophe replaces "19")

## Style & Grammar

### Active voice (preferred)
- PASSIVE: "The file was saved by the system."
- ACTIVE: "The system saved the file." (clearer, shorter)

### Parallel structure in lists
- WRONG: "The app can save files, loading data, and to export reports."
- RIGHT: "The app can save files, load data, and export reports."

### Subject-verb agreement
- "The team is" (AmE — team as unit) / "The team are" (BrE — team as individuals)
- "Neither the manager nor the employees were" (verb agrees with nearest subject)
- "Each of the files has" (each = singular)
- "Data is" (modern usage) / "Data are" (traditional — both acceptable)
